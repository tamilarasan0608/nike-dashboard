Step 1 · Raw Materials
38 supplier factories in Vietnam provide textiles, rubber, foam, adhesives, and components sourced from regional and international suppliers.
Step 2 · Components
Fabric cutting, sole production (rubber, EVA foam), and component assembly (eyelets, laces, padding) with quality testing at each phase.
Step 3 · Assembly
142 finished goods factories handle stitching, gluing, assembly, and finishing. Skilled workforce performs intricate detail work.
Step 4 · Finishing
Final QA inspection, packaging (boxes, labels, hang tags), and inventory management in Vietnam warehouses.
Step 5 · Logistics
Deep-water port infrastructure in southern Vietnam (Ho Chi Minh City). Containerization and shipping to global distribution centers.
Step 6 · Distribution
US port entry (LA, Long Beach, NY). Customs clearance, duty assessment, US distribution centers, retail stores, and online fulfillment.

Broader Industry Context

Manufacturing exposure by country — Nike vs Adidas vs Puma (% of total footwear production)

The "China+1" Strategy Backfired: Nike and peers spent a decade moving out of China into Vietnam, Indonesia, and Cambodia to reduce geopolitical risk. The April 2025 tariffs impose 46–49% duties on those exact same countries — penalizing the diversification that was supposed to reduce risk.
⚠️ Reshoring to the US is not economically viable. Labor costs are 10–15× higher than Vietnam. A shoe costing $18 in Vietnam would cost $60–$80 to make in the US, pushing retail prices above $200 for current $115 models.
